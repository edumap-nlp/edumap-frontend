# Quantum Game Theory

## Game Theory Models
### Classical Models
#### Classical Volunteer’s Dilemma
The traditional form of the volunteer’s dilemma with classical strategies.
#### Mixed Strategies
Strategies where players probabilistically decide to volunteer.
#### Nash Equilibrium
A strategy profile where no player can unilaterally improve their payoff.
#### Symmetric Nash Equilibria
Equilibria where all players adopt the same strategy.
#### Unique Symmetric Nash Equilibrium
A single symmetric equilibrium in the classical game with mixed strategies.
#### Pareto Optimality
A condition where no player can improve their payoff without reducing another's payoff.
#### Cost-Sharing Variant
A version of the dilemma where volunteering costs are shared among volunteers.
#### Rational and Self-Interested Players
Assumption that players aim to maximize their own payoffs.

### Quantum Models
#### Quantum Volunteer’s Dilemma
A quantum variant of the volunteer’s dilemma using quantum strategies.
#### Quantum Prisoner’s Dilemma
A quantum game where quantum strategies resolve classical dilemmas.
#### Quantum Battle-of-the-Sexes
A quantum game resolving multiple equilibria in its classical version.
#### Quantum Chicken Game
A quantum game achieving higher payoffs than classical strategies.
#### Quantum Parrondo’s Games
Quantum games where losing strategies can lead to winning outcomes.
#### Quantum Minority Game
A game where players benefit by being in the minority, extended to quantum strategies.
#### Quantum Market Games
Games modeling market interactions with quantum strategies.
#### Quantum Cooperative Games
Games exploring cooperation using quantum strategies.
