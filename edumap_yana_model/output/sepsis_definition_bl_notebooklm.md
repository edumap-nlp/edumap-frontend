# Sepsis Definition

## Sepsis Diagnosis Challenges
### High Rate of Missed Diagnoses
- 60-75% of patients meeting sepsis criteria are undiagnosed at discharge.
- Only 25% of patients received an explicit sepsis diagnosis.

### Discrepancies in Diagnosis Criteria
- Explicit diagnosis relies on ICD-9 codes; implicit combines infection and organ dysfunction codes.
- Sepsis-3 criteria poorly predict discharge diagnoses.

### Risk Factors for Non-Diagnosed Patients
- 19% to 36% of undiagnosed patients had risk factors for harm from fluid resuscitation.
- Common noninfectious diagnoses include poisoning and acute respiratory failure.

## Sepsis as a Global Health Issue
### Prevalence and Mortality
- Sepsis causes approximately 5.3 million deaths annually worldwide.
- Leading cause of death in U.S. ICUs, with high inpatient costs.

### Efforts for Improvement
- U.S. CMS established SEP-1 core measure for sepsis care quality.
- Emphasis on timely antibiotic administration and early identification.

## Treatment Protocols and Risks
### Fluid Resuscitation Concerns
- Optimal resuscitation methods for sepsis remain uncertain.
- Overly aggressive fluid administration can lead to adverse outcomes.

### Implications for Patient Management
- Initial clinical parameters may not accurately reflect sepsis diagnosis.
- Careful consideration needed for patients with comorbid conditions.

## Research and Future Directions
### Need for Further Studies
- Randomized trials comparing fluid therapy approaches are necessary.
- Ongoing research on sepsis management and treatment strategies is recommended.

### Limitations of Current Findings
- Study findings may not be generalizable due to retrospective data reliance.
- Potential misattribution biases from ICD coding could affect results.
