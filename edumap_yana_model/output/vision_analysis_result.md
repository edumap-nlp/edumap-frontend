# 1. Background

## Introduction
Sepsis is a major cause of mortality in hospitals worldwide. The study focuses on the effectiveness of sepsis resuscitation criteria in emergency departments (ED) and the potential over-treatment of patients who do not meet explicit sepsis diagnosis criteria at discharge.

# 2. Study Design & Patient Cohort

## Methods
- **Study Type**: Retrospective observational cohort study.
- **Population**: Adult ED patients (age ≥ 18 years) in the U.S. upper Midwest.
- **Time Frame**: January 2007 to October 2015.
- **Criteria**: Sepsis-3 criteria, including SOFA score ≥ 2 and administration of IV antibiotics within 24 hours.

## Data Collection
- **Data Sources**: EPIC electronic medical records, ICD-9 codes.
- **Analysis Tools**: Microsoft Excel, Stata v15.1.
- **Outcome Measures**: Diagnosis of sepsis at discharge, risk factors for harm from fluid resuscitation.

# 3. Clinical Outcomes

## Results
- **Patient Cohort**: 4,060 patients received IV antibiotics, 3,121 met Sepsis-3 criteria.
- **Diagnosis at Discharge**:
  - Explicit sepsis: 25.1% (95% CI: 23.6–6.7)
  - Implicit sepsis: 47.8% (95% CI: 46.1–49.6)
- **Sepsis-3 Plus Shock**:
  - Explicit sepsis: 47.9% (95% CI: 44.8–50.9)
  - Implicit sepsis: 61.7% (95% CI: 58.7–64.6)

## Patient Characteristics
- **Sample Size**: 
  - Sepsis-3: 3,121
  - Sepsis-3 plus shock: 1,032
- **Age**: 
  - Sepsis-3: 59 (±17.5) years
  - Sepsis-3 plus shock: 60 (±17) years
- **Gender**: 
  - Female: 36.6% in both groups

## Risk Factors and Outcomes
- **Risk Factors for Harm**: At least one risk factor in 38.6% of Sepsis-3 patients.
- **ICU Length of Stay**: 
  - Sepsis-3: 0.6 days (IQR: 0, 3.1)
  - Sepsis-3 plus shock: 0 (IQR: 0, 1.8)
- **Mortality**: 
  - Sepsis-3: 27.1% (95% CI: 8.7)
  - Sepsis-3 plus shock: 16.3% (95% CI: 15.6)

# 4. Discussion

## Conclusions
A significant number of patients meeting Sepsis-3 criteria were not diagnosed with sepsis at discharge, indicating potential over-treatment and exposure to unnecessary interventions.

## Limitations
- Single-site study limits generalizability.
- Retrospective design may introduce bias.

## Implications
The findings suggest a need for more nuanced resuscitation strategies and highlight the importance of accurate sepsis diagnosis to avoid unnecessary treatments.
