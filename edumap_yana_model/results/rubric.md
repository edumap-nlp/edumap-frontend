# Manual Evaluation Rubric for Summary Quality

To evaluate the quality of generated summaries, we designed a structured manual assessment framework. Each output is evaluated across multiple dimensions using a 0–5 scoring system, where **5 indicates near-perfect performance**.

---

## 1. Conceptual Accuracy

**Definition:**  
Measures whether the summary correctly reflects the key concepts and findings of the original document without introducing errors or misinterpretations.

**Scoring Criteria:**
- **5** – All key concepts are accurate; no factual errors or hallucinations  
- **4** – Mostly accurate; only minor inaccuracies  
- **3** – Some inaccuracies or incomplete understanding  
- **2** – Multiple important errors  
- **1** – Major misunderstandings  
- **0** – Completely incorrect or misleading  

**Full-score standard:**  
The summary correctly captures the main conclusions, such as the discrepancy between clinical criteria and discharge diagnosis, and does not distort or fabricate information.


## 2. Coverage of Key Ideas

**Definition:**  
Evaluates how comprehensively the summary captures the essential components of the original document.

**Scoring Criteria:**
- **5** – Covers all major sections and key ideas  
- **4** – Minor omissions  
- **3** – Covers approximately half of the key content  
- **2** – Significant omissions  
- **1** – Only a few key points included  
- **0** – Largely irrelevant or missing  

**Full-score standard:**  
The summary includes all major elements, such as:
- Background / motivation  
- Methods / study design  
- Key results  
- Implications or conclusions  


## 3. Hierarchical Organization

**Definition:**  
Assesses the clarity and logical structure of the summary, especially whether it is organized in a meaningful and interpretable hierarchy.

**Scoring Criteria:**
- **5** – Clear and well-structured hierarchy; logically organized  
- **4** – Mostly clear structure  
- **3** – Some structure but partially disorganized  
- **2** – Weak or inconsistent structure  
- **1** – Minimal structure  
- **0** – No clear organization  

**Full-score standard:**  
The summary uses clear sectioning (e.g., headings or logical grouping) and organizes information in a way that supports understanding and navigation (e.g., suitable for mind maps).


## 4. Usefulness for Studying

**Definition:**  
Measures how useful the summary is for learning, reviewing, or presentation purposes.

**Scoring Criteria:**
- **5** – Highly useful; clear, concise, and easy to study from  
- **4** – Generally useful  
- **3** – Moderately useful  
- **2** – Limited usefulness  
- **1** – Difficult to use for studying  
- **0** – Not useful  

**Full-score standard:**  
The summary is concise, readable, and effectively highlights important points, making it suitable for exam review, presentations, or quick comprehension.

---


## Overall Scoring

Each dimension is scored from **0 to 5**.  
The total score can be computed as **Average Score (0–5)** (Mean across dimensions)
